Knot theory and number theory run on parallel tracks: primes behave like knots, quadratic residues like linking numbers, and zeta functions arise on both sides by the same sum-into-product mechanism. One story — intersection counts, what happens when they vanish, and the zeta functions that record it all — told simultaneously in both languages, using the smallest genuine examples throughout: the Hopf link and the pair (5, 13), the unlink and the pair (5, 29); the Borromean rings and the triple (13, 61, 937).

Each prime is secretly a circle: the Galois group of the finite field Fp is Ẑ — exactly the profinite fundamental group of the circle S¹ — so Spec Fp is a circle in disguise. And the space it sits in, Spec Z, behaves 3-dimensionally — étale cohomological dimension 3. That is the load-bearing claim of this whole app, so here is what stands behind it, in the open rather than under a cursor. Artin–Verdier duality (1964): on the étale site of Spec OK there is a trace isomorphism H³(Spec OK, Gm) ≅ Q/Z, and the pairing of Hⁱ(F) against Ext³⁻ⁱ(F, Gm) into Q/Z is perfect for constructible F. That is Poincaré duality in top dimension 3, with Gm as the orientation sheaf — and that is the precise sense in which Spec Z behaves like a closed oriented 3-manifold. Two pieces of fine print, since a manifold theorist will ask: Spec Z is not compact, and the real place contributes 2-torsion corrections; both are handled by compactifying Spec Z (adding the archimedean place). Cited, not computed — Artin–Verdier, Woods Hole 1964; Mazur, Ann. Sci. ENS 6 (1973). A circle inside a 3-space is a knot — Mazur's observation, and the reason the table below is a dictionary rather than a coincidence.

The congruence p ≡ 1 (mod 4) tames ramification at 2 and ∞ in the quadratic resolvents — it plays the role of orientability. The taming does not propagate up the tower — the why-box below spells out the S ∪ {∞} convention. One asymmetry worth noting: a knot bounds many Seifert surfaces and every construction must be checked independent of the choice; the quadratic resolvent is unique, so at this level well-definedness is automatic on the arithmetic side. The asymmetry does not survive the climb: one rung up, the Rédei symbol buys its well-definedness with a normalization condition on the certificate, and at the quadruple level with a positivity condition — the rejection taxonomy the Triples cockpit puts on screen, and the positivity check step 3 of Quadruples runs live.
Over Q there is no rank-r tower mirroring the knot side's Zr-cover. For ℓ ∉ S, the Galois group of the maximal abelian ℓ-extension of Q unramified outside S ∪ {∞} is ∏ᵢ Z/ℓeᵢ with eᵢ = v_ℓ(pᵢ − 1) — finite; for S = {13, 61}, ℓ = 2 it is Z/4 × Z/4. The ∪ {∞} is a convention that must be said, and it bites exactly at ℓ = 2: complex conjugation lands on the order-2 element (2,2), so the totally real (∞-unramified) tower is the strictly smaller Z/4 × Z/2 — p ≡ 1 (mod 4) tames ∞ for the resolvent Q(√p) only, not higher (the quartic subfield of Q(ζ₁₃) is already imaginary). So fS lives over the finite deck group's ring — Z_ℓ[[u₁,…,u_r]] with (1+uᵢ)^(ℓ^eᵢ) = 1 imposed — while Iwasawa's own Λ = Z_ℓ[[T]] series runs over the cyclotomic tower, whose deck group really is Z_ℓ, but which is ramified at ℓ (Morishita, Knots and Primes, ch. 9–12, 1st ed.).
One thread, told twice.
An intersection count (linking number / Legendre symbol), symmetric because its two values are the two ends of a single global object (an arc / an adèlic product). When the count vanishes everywhere, the intersection object can be closed up (a closed curve / the Rédei extension) and its interaction with the third participant becomes the triple invariant (μ̄(123) / [p₁,p₂,p₃]) — well defined exactly because the lower level vanished. And a polynomial (ΔL / fS) records the hierarchy as its surviving low-order coefficients, while being — by Milnor–Turaev and the Main Conjecture respectively — the zeta function of the relevant cover: a sum over iterates or ideals, resummed as a product over orbits or primes, eigenvalues or characters — the pairs the ζ ↔ Δ boxes track, tab by tab, to the Zeta tab.
